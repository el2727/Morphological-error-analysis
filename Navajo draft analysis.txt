Navajo case study, error analysis: 
From Reichard, 1951:


* “Speakers often fail to distinguish dual and plural, using the same forms for both.”


We have the exact same case with the model output:


gold:
y-i-ghááh               woo-h-kai          V;REAL;PFV/PRF;2;PL


hypo:
y-i-ghááh               woo'-áázh        <- valid 2nd person duoplural, but 2nd person plural is target


* “The greatest difficulty of analysis is due to the large number of overlapping forms and to the many functions performed by a single prefix of the type CV (e.g. yi- is a 3rd person object and agent, progressive, momentary, present and perfective aspects, etc.)”.
